02 // Core Framework

8-Layer Prompt Architecture

Foundational Full Architecture Template
# ROLE → CONTEXT → OBJECTIVE → CONSTRAINTS → AESTHETIC → PLANNING → OUTPUT → REFINEMENT ROLE You are a [expert role]. CONTEXT Product: [name] Platform: [mobile/web] Audience: [who] OBJECTIVE [One clear sentence of success] Success criteria: - [criterion 1] - [criterion 2] CONSTRAINTS - Mobile-first - WCAG AA accessibility - 60fps animation budget AESTHETIC - [visual style keywords] PLANNING 1. Define information architecture 2. Define layout and grid 3. Validate accessibility OUTPUT FORMAT Generate: 1. [file type] 2. [instructions] REFINEMENT - Critique for clarity - Refine once for structure - Refine once for polish
☑ Quality Checklist — Architecture
Role defined — who is the AI acting as?
Goal clear — one sentence maximum
Success criteria stated explicitly
Constraints listed (accessibility, performance)
Output format requested

Top 5 Recommended Mode Chains

🦅 Eagle → 🦫 Beaver → 🐜 Ant Build AI Content System
▶
# Build AI Content System Step 1 (Eagle): Think like an eagle flying high above the landscape. Explain the long-term strategy for building an AI content system. What are the core components? How do they connect? Step 2 (Beaver): Think like a beaver building a dam. Design the system architecture step by step. Create modular components for content generation, storage, and delivery. Step 3 (Ant): Think like an ant. Break this into the smallest possible implementation steps. Each step should be completable in one session.
Best Use-Case

Building automated content pipelines, AI-powered blogs, or content generation systems that require strategic planning followed by systematic implementation.

🦉 Owl → 🐬 Dolphin → 🐘 Elephant Solve Complex Problem
▶
# Solve Complex Problem Step 1 (Owl): Think like an owl — slow, observant and analytical. Examine this problem from multiple perspectives. Identify hidden factors most people overlook. Step 2 (Dolphin): Think like a dolphin — curious, playful and inventive. Generate creative solutions that most people wouldn't consider. Think outside conventional approaches. Step 3 (Elephant): Think like an elephant with a powerful memory. Connect solutions to insights from psychology, economics, science, or history. Find analogies and patterns from other fields.
Best Use-Case

Innovative product design, breakthrough feature ideas, or solving entrenched problems that require both analytical depth and creative thinking.

🐇 Rabbit → 🦅 Eagle → 🐜 Ant Brainstorm Channel/Product
▶
# Brainstorm Channel or Product Step 1 (Rabbit): Multiply this idea into 10 different variations. Change the angle, change the audience, change the format. Step 2 (Eagle): Think like an eagle flying high above the landscape. Identify which variations have the strongest long-term potential. Analyze market fit and scalability. Step 3 (Ant): Break your chosen direction into actionable steps. What needs to happen first? What's the MVP?
Best Use-Case

New product ideation, marketing channel selection, or exploring different business model variations before committing to a direction.

🦫 Beaver → 🐜 Ant → 🦉 Owl Design Workflow/Automation
▶
# Design Workflow or Automation Step 1 (Beaver): Think like a beaver building a dam. Design a practical system that solves this problem step by step. Map out the core workflow components. Step 2 (Ant): Break into smallest possible steps. Each step should be a discrete action that can be automated. Consider error handling and edge cases. Step 3 (Owl): Review for gaps and inefficiencies. Validate the workflow makes logical sense. Identify potential bottlenecks.
Best Use-Case

Building automation scripts, designing approval workflows, or creating SOPs that need both implementation structure and quality review.

🦉 Owl → 🐘 Elephant → 🦅 Eagle Validate Business Idea
▶
# Validate Business Idea Step 1 (Owl): Conduct deep analysis of this business idea. Examine market size, competition, and feasibility. Identify risks and assumptions that need testing. Step 2 (Elephant): Research similar successes and failures. Connect to case studies from other industries. What patterns can inform this idea? Step 3 (Eagle): Evaluate long-term strategic viability. Is this sustainable? Scalable? Defensible? What's the 10,000-foot view?
Best Use-Case

Validating startup ideas, evaluating new business ventures, or assessing strategic decisions before major investment.

☑ Quality Checklist — Thinking Modes
Mode selected based on task type
For complex tasks, modes chained in logical order
Each mode serves specific purpose in chain
Output from one feeds into next

Ultra-Modern Gen-Z Design Combos

🫧 Glass + Bento
Best for: Dashboard interfaces, data visualization
glassmorphism bento grid dark-mode native
Psychology: Creates depth perception and hierarchy while maintaining information density. Glass effects reduce visual weight while bento grids organize content into scannable chunks.
💥 Brutal + Neon
Best for: Landing pages, bold brands
brutalist ui neon accent kinetic typography
Psychology: Creates urgency and attention through high contrast. Brutalism cuts through noise while neon guides focus to key elements.
🌊 Liquid + Ambient
Best for: Hero sections, immersive experiences
liquid gradient ambient motion dark-mode native
Psychology: Evokes emotion and flow. Liquid gradients create organic feel while ambient motion keeps the interface alive without overwhelming.
📰 Editorial + Bento
Best for: Content platforms, blogs, portfolios
editorial layout bento grid progressive disclosure
Psychology: Combines editorial authority with modern organization. Creates content hierarchy while maintaining visual interest across the page.
✨ Micro + Skeleton
Best for: Apps, data-heavy interfaces
micro-interactions skeleton loading dark-mode native
Psychology: Reduces perceived wait time and keeps users engaged. Micro-interactions provide feedback while skeletons maintain layout stability.
🚀 Full Immersive
Best for: Marketing sites, product launches
kinetic typography liquid gradient micro-interactions ambient motion
Psychology: Maximum engagement through sensory richness. Creates memorable experiences that differentiate brands in competitive markets.
